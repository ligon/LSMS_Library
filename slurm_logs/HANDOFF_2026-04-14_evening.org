* Test results — 7 failed, triaged

** Bucket A: atol too strict (2)

*** =fct= — trivial fix
- Evidence: =Donuts= column, 3/15 rows, max |Δ| = 7.1e-15 > atol 0.
- Cause: FP noise from the nutrient-table lookup.
- Fix: in FEATURE_SPECS, bump =fct= atol from =0.0= to =1e-12=.

*** =nutrition= — investigate before masking
- Evidence: =Energy= column, 2/23679 rows, max |Δ| = *8200*, mean |Δ|
  = 0.381.
- Cause UNKNOWN: two specific outlier rows with massive energy
  difference, while the other 23,677 agree to ~0.4 kcal (as expected
  from the 2018/2019 unit-filter tightening noted in Round 2 report).
- Do NOT just bump atol to 10,000 — those two rows need eyeballing
  first. Likely candidates: a food item whose =fct= row moved between
  replication and API, or a unit-conversion bug on a specific
  quantity.

** Bucket B: NaN alignment (4)

All four fail via the =only_one_na.any()= assertion — meaning a
specific small count of rows has a value on one side and NaN on the
other. Not systematic drift.

*** =household_characteristics= — 1 row
- Evidence: =log HSize= column, 1 row NaN-mismatch out of 23,575
  common rows.
- Likely cause: A single HH where =age_handler()= nulled all members'
  ages, producing HSize=0 → log HSize=NaN on the API side, while the
  replication (pre-age_handler) kept a positive HSize.
- Fix: verify by pulling that one =(t,i)= pair; if confirmed, either
  drop HSize=0 rows from comparison or widen the transform.

*** =shocks= — 6 rows
- Evidence: =Shock= column, 6 rows NaN-mismatch out of 26,669 common
  rows.
- Likely cause: 6 shock categories that exist on one side's shock
  spelling mapping but not the other.
- Fix: pull the 6 rows, see which shock strings are NaN, add to
  =categorical_mapping/shocks= or equivalent.

*** =food_prices= — 4 rows
- Evidence: =market= column (the =market='Region'= join output), 4
  rows NaN-mismatch out of 346,117.
- Likely cause: 4 specific =(t,i,v)= clusters whose region is
  recorded on one side (via cover-page join) and NA on the other.
- Fix: pull the 4 clusters; probably a cover-page gap in 1-2 waves.

*** =food_quantities= — 10 rows
- Evidence: =quantity_home= column, 10 rows NaN-mismatch out of
  346,117.
- Likely cause: 10 rows where one side treats "quantity not asked"
  as NaN and the other as 0 (or vice versa).
- Fix: pull and diagnose.

** Bucket C: systematic schema mismatch (1)

*** =household_roster= — ALL 35,493 rows differ on =Sex=
- Evidence: non-numeric equality fails on every row. Clearly
  dtype/encoding level, not a data drift.
- Hypothesis 1: API returns =Sex= as =pd.Categorical= which =.astype(str)=
  produces differently than the replication's string series.
- Hypothesis 2: =M/F= on one side, =Male/Female= on the other — the
  test's current =_tx_household_roster= doesn't normalize.
- Fix: dump 3 rows' =Sex= values from each side with =.dtype=, and
  extend =_tx_household_roster= with whatever transform makes them
  match.

* Performance mystery (not blocking)

12 of 15 tests took 2,200–2,577 s. The 3 fast ones (=locality=,
=fct=, =household_roster=) finished in 3–5 s. The correlation: the
12 slow ones all use =market='Region'=, which triggers
=_add_market_index()= at query time; the 3 fast ones don't.

When 12 pytest-xdist workers hit the market-join path concurrently,
they serialized to ~37 min — suggests shared lock or cache
contention. Worth investigating separately; not blocking this test.

Likely culprits:
- Shared parquet read through DVCFileSystem / =load_dataframe_with_dvc=
  with a file-lock;
- =_add_market_index= re-loading =cluster_features= 12× against the
  same cache file;
- Memory pressure + paging (12 workers, each ~500 MB resident).

Instrumentation to try next: =py-spy dump= on one of the stuck
workers during the plateau, or =pyinstrument= via the =profiling=
skill.
