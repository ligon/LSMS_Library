* The organizing principle

Before ordering anything: *the GitHub backlog should hold exactly what the
coverage matrix cannot.*

The matrix now enumerates cells, waves, and countries --- systematically,
refreshably, and with evidence.  Any issue that /is/ a cell ("EthiopiaRHS
=plot_features= needs R2--R4", "India =housing= needs decoding") is
*duplicate bookkeeping*: the Phase-1 sweep will re-derive it, sized and
sourced, whether or not an issue exists.  Maintaining both is how a backlog
becomes a museum.

So the division of labour is:

| the *matrix* owns | *which* cells are missing / broken / blocked --- the WHAT |
| the *backlog* owns | /why/ a cell is hard, cross-cutting bugs, and *everything the matrix cannot see* |

That last clause is not a throwaway.  The matrix has *known blind spots*, and
they are exactly where issues earn their keep:

- =#588= --- Uganda =earnings= is *sane cold, broken warm*.  The matrix is a
  cold build.  It /structurally cannot/ see this.
- =#589= --- GhanaLSS currency labels.  The grader does not check them.

*Track what the instrument cannot see.  Let the instrument track the rest.*
This shrinks the backlog structurally rather than by grinding.

* The severity rule

Order by *harm*, not by effort or age:

1. *Silently wrong* --- the user gets a wrong number and does not know.  The
   only /unrecoverable/ class: it propagates into published work.
2. *Silently missing* --- the user gets less data than they think.
3. *Loudly broken* --- it raises.  Annoying; harmless.  It cannot corrupt an
   analysis, because there is no analysis.
4. *Untidy* --- noise, dead code, ergonomics.

A crash is a gift.  A quiet wrong answer is a liability.

** 1a. =#503= Guyana --- *this is data corruption, and it is the worst item on the board*

=i = [ED, HH]= *conflates distinct households*.  The source is at
=(ED, HH, SN)= granularity:

: COVERN.dta   1807 rows | 1502 unique (ED,HH) | 1807 unique (ED,HH,SN)
: ROSTERN.dta            | 1495 unique (ED,HH) | 1789 unique (ED,HH,SN)

So roughly *300 distinct households are silently merged into others.*  This is
not missing data --- it is /wrong data that looks fine/.  Every Guyana number we
have ever produced is suspect, and nothing in the matrix flags it, because the
table builds clean and passes every sanity check.

It is the purest instance of the session's theme: *=sane= is not =blessed=.*

* One meta-observation

Of the ~29 open issues, a large fraction are *not defects at all* --- they are
cells, decisions, or acquisitions that were filed as issues because there was
nowhere else to put them.  Now there is.  The backlog should get *structurally*
smaller as the matrix takes over the bookkeeping, and what remains should be
harder and more interesting: real bugs, real blind spots, and real decisions.
