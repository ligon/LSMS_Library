* Legend

- *BOTH* — both libraries cover the area for this country.
- *OURS-ONLY* — LSMS_Library covers it; the WB panel constructs nothing.
- *THEIRS-ONLY* — the WB panel constructs it; we have no feature.
- *NEITHER* — neither side covers it for this country.
- superscript =a= — *theirs is dominantly aggregated* (=collapse=/aggregating
  =egen=/factor index/median-price valuation). On a =THEIRS-ONLY^a= cell the
  parity move is "expose item-level feature + add a =transformations= fn",
  NOT "build their aggregate as a feature". On a =BOTH^a= cell we already
  hold the item-level data and reach their aggregate at query time — the =a=
  flags that their *stored* form is the aggregate, ours is the items.

* Reading notes / caveats

- *Area 3 (HH composition) is BOTH^a everywhere*: =hh_size= is item-ish but
  =hh_dependency_ratio= (their dominant construct) is a ratio of HH sums. We
  cover the area via =household_characteristics= (runtime-derived from
  =household_roster=), so parity is already correct — the dependency ratio is
  a transform over our roster, not a stored column. No gap.
- *Area 11 (Ag valuations) is BOTH^a, but our coverage is thin/indirect.*
  Our "feature" here is =food_prices= (runtime-derived from =food_acquired=) —
  it is *valuation logic only* (Expenditure/Quantity unit values), and it
  values *consumed food*, not *plot harvest / seed / fertilizer / hired
  labor*. Theirs values the agricultural production side via median-price
  imputation. So Area 11 is BOTH only in the loose sense "both compute some
  valuation"; for the *agricultural* valuations it is effectively a gap that
  is *downstream* of the Area 9/10 item-feature gaps and is handled in
  GAP_RANKING as a transformations-layer follow-on, never a data feature.
- *Area 13 (Assets) BOTH^a*: we store item-level asset ownership (=assets=);
  they store only the =ag_asset_index= / =hh_asset_index= PCA factor scores.
  Full parity = add a =transformations= fn that runs the PCA, NOT store an
  index. Already item-level on our side, so no data-layer gap.
- *Area 14 (Consumption/welfare) BOTH^a*: we hold item-level
  =food_acquired= (+ =nonfood_expenditures= for Nigeria/Uganda); they hold
  only =totcons_{LCU,USD}= / =cons_quint= taken from a precomputed WB
  consumption aggregate. =totcons= and =cons_quint= are transforms over our
  item data — no data-layer gap, optional welfare-aggregate transform.
- *Area 6 (Anthropometry) vs 6b (Nutrition-intake)* — two distinct areas:
  - *6 Anthropometry* = body measures (weight, height) + WHO z-scores
    (=haz06/waz06/whz06/bmiz06=, =wasting=). Their z-scores are item-level
    (per child); the *score* is a reference-table transform. We have NO
    anthropometry feature, so this is THEIRS-ONLY wherever they build it
    (ETH/MWI/NGA/TZA/UGA = 5 countries; Mali/Niger have none). Genuine
    item-level gap → build an =anthropometry= feature (GAP 5).
  - *6b Nutrition-intake* = nutrient intake from consumption × food-composition
    tables (our =nutrition= for ETH/UGA + =fct= for UGA). They construct NO
    nutrient intake (their HDDS is diet *diversity*, filed under Area 18).
    OURS-ONLY — a place we are ahead; nothing to build, no overlap to compare.
- *Area 18 (Food security)*: their only construct is =HDDS= (a dietary-
  diversity *aggregate*), built in ALL 7 countries (=egen rowtotal= +
  =collapse (max)=; verified per-country =.do=) → BOTH^a everywhere. We carry
  true experience-scale food security (FIES, rCSI, months_food_inadequate);
  HDDS is NOT an experience scale, so where we want it, it is a transform over
  item-level diet recall, never a stored feature. The "both" is loose —
  different constructs (their diet-diversity count vs our experience scales)
  sharing one area.
- *Area 19 (Community food prices) — superscript =*=*: a distinct grain
  *(t, v, j)* (cluster × food-item × time) from a *community/market price
  questionnaire*. The WB panel does NOT survey community prices at all — its
  =crop_price_EA= is a *median of household sale unit-values* laddered
  EA→admin→national (=programs.do:31-89=), an HH-derived imputation, not a
  surveyed community price. So at this grain they are effectively absent →
  OURS-ONLY. =*= = *we have the raw data, mostly unwired*: community price
  modules exist across LSMS-ISA (Uganda =csec3*=, Malawi =com_cc=, Nigeria
  =sectc*= Community, Ethiopia ESS), Tanzania has a =community_prices.py=
  starter, but there is no harmonized cross-country =community_prices= feature
  yet. Build target: =community_prices (t,v,j)= with =j= on the SAME
  =harmonize_food= labels as =food_acquired= (and the GAP-1 =crop_production=
  crop labels) so consumed foods, grown crops, and surveyed prices all join.
