*natural grain*, each its own narrow-or-wide DataFrame, NEVER a wide mirror

*Hard rule restated*: no aggregate / index / ratio / median-price valuation

*Label unification (maintainer steer)*: BOTH the item axis =j= AND the unit

=crop_production= (GAP 1) and =community_prices= (GAP C):

crop-only or price-only label schemes. This is parity *Unit #0* (the label

* GAP 1 — Crop production / harvest  [THEIRS-ONLY^a × all 7]  (HIGHEST)

Why #1: present for all 7, agriculturally central, and it is the *upstream
item layer* that Areas 11 (harvest/yield/seed valuations) and parts of 9's
own aggregates all depend on. Closing it unblocks the most downstream parity.

*Tasteful decomposition — ONE item feature:*

- =crop_production= at natural grain *(t, i, plot, crop)* — one row per crop
  grown on a plot, with =crop= on the SHARED =harmonize_food= labels (see
  Label unification, so crops join consumed foods + community prices),
  carrying the *reported* fields only:
  - reported harvest quantity + native unit (=Quantity=, =u=),
  - reported quantity sold + sale value (=Quantity_sold=, =Value_sold=),
  - planting / harvest-end month (=item= dates),
  - intercropped / perennial flags.
  Keep it *narrow and item-level*. Do NOT bake plot area, crop-group dummies,
  shares, or value into it.

*Everything aggregate stays in =transformations.py=* (=add-transformations-fn=):
- =harvest_kg= : unit→kg conversion then sum over native units per (plot,crop).
- =yield_kg= : =harvest_kg / plot_area= (joins =plot_features=).
- =share_kg_sold= : =Quantity_sold / harvest_kg=.
- =main_crop=, =maincrop_valueshare=, =contains_crop_*=, =share_value_*= :
  value-rank + crop-group rollups over the item rows.

*.do to consult*: harvest sum =ETH_ESS1.do:256= + =Append_ETH.do:241,296=;
=NGA_GHS1.do:191-292= (harvest_kg / sold / yield / main_crop / contains_crop);
=TZA_NPS1.do:205-221=; crop-group programs =main_crop_def= programs.do:110-137.

* Parity-loop note

GAPs 1→2→3 are the load-bearing item-feature builds (they also unblock GAP 7
valuations and parts of GAP 1's own aggregates). GAPs 4-5 are small,
self-contained item features. GAPs 6-8 + below-the-line are
=transformations.py= work over data we already hold. Suggested loop order:
1 → 2 → 3 (item layer) → 7 (valuation transform) → 4 → 5 → 6 → 8.
