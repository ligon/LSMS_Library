#+title: Contents

The Ethiopian Rural Household Survey (ERHS) is a longitudinal survey of
rural households in Ethiopia, distinct from the World Bank ESS /
LSMS-ISA waves carried in the separate =Ethiopia= module.  It is the
data behind a well-known price-risk / welfare literature (Dercon,
Krishnan, and collaborators).  This declaration tracks the
library-side enhancement requested in GH #271.

* Survey Program

Run jointly by the Department of Economics, Addis Ababa University
(AAU); the Centre for the Study of African Economies (CSAE), Oxford;
and the International Food Policy Research Institute (IFPRI).  Initial
fieldwork in 1989 covered 6--7 villages in Central/Southern Ethiopia;
in 1994 the sample was expanded to ~1,494 households across 15
villages (=peasant associations=) and revisited repeatedly.

Eight rounds are declared:

| Wave  | Round | Fieldwork           | Notes                                  |
|-------+-------+---------------------+----------------------------------------|
| 1989  | --    | 1989                | 6--7 villages; pre-expansion instrument |

** Access / Terms of Use

Contrary to GH #271's initial assumption, ERHS is *not* DUA-gated.
Files are publicly downloadable without an account or API token via
the Dataverse data-access API:

: curl -L "https://dataverse.harvard.edu/api/access/datafile/<id>?format=original"

=format=original= returns the original Stata =.dta= for ingested
tabular files (verified).  The IFPRI Dataverse Terms of Use require
acknowledging the source and are non-commercial in spirit; cite IFPRI
/ AAU / CSAE and the DOI above.  Researchers are encouraged to
coordinate with AAU Economics (contact historically Stefan Dercon).
