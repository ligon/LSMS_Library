#+TITLE: Guyana — country notes & known data issues

* Household and cluster identity (READ FIRST)

The household key is the *three-level* =(ED, SN, HH)= and the sampling cluster is
the *two-level* =(ED, SN)=.  =SN= is the ED sample-segment serial — spelled
=ed_smpl= in =HHCHAR.dta= / =WEIGHTID.dta=, and =SN= in
=COVERN/ROSTERN/EDUCN=.  So =i = "ED-SN-HH"= (e.g. ="5-194-2"=) and
=v = "ED-SN"= (e.g. ="5-194"=).

*ED numbers are reused across segments.*  They are NOT unique on their own:

| ED | SN  | Region | Sector | households |
|----+-----+--------+--------+------------|
|  5 | 194 |      4 | urban  |         12 |
|  5 | 702 |     10 | rural  |         12 |

Those are two different enumeration districts that happen to share the number 5.

Until GH #323 the config declared =i: [ED, HH]= and =v: ED=.  Both are wrong,
and both were *silently* wrong: the framework's =_normalize_dataframe_index=
collapses a non-unique declared index with =groupby().first()=, so 248 =(ED,HH)=
buckets fused 542 distinct real households holding 2,381 of 7,827 people (30.4%),
and one household's members were kept while another's were thrown away.  The
survey's own id proves the key: =COVERN.NEWID == ED*100000 + SN*100 + HH= for
1807/1807 rows, all unique.

Row counts before → after the fix:

| table                | before |  after |
|----------------------+--------+--------|
| household_roster     |  6,939 |  7,827 |
| individual_education |  4,137 |  4,633 |
| housing              |  1,508 |  1,817 |
| sample               |  1,502 |  1,807 |
| interview_date       |  1,502 |  1,807 |
| assets               | 10,345 | 11,227 |
| cluster_features     |    130 |    168 |

Enforced by =tests/test_guyana_index_uniqueness.py= (pinned row counts, the ED-5
de-fusion, and a *cold-build* no-collapse-warning check).  A warm-cache check
would pass vacuously — the collapse is baked into the cache, so the warning
never fires twice.  Prose here is documentation; the test is the enforcement.

* Declared aggregation / drop policies

These are the places where a duplicate is resolved by an *explicit, declared*
rule instead of the framework's silent =first()=.  All live in
=1992/_/mapping.py= as =df_edit= hooks.

** housing — one household DROPPED (class-2: loudly missing)
Exactly one duplicate survives the correct 3-level key:
=(ed_dvsn=123, ed_smpl=722, smpl_hh=6)= — a duplicated record in the *source*
(=WEIGHTID.dta= carries it too), whose two rows share =newid= (12372206) and
=hhsize= (3) but disagree on ~35 columns including =totexp= (6,345 vs 25,843) and
wall material (=bldg3= 1=Wood vs 3=Wood & Concrete).  There is no principled way
to choose, so *both rows are dropped* with a warning naming the household.  It is
therefore absent from =housing= but present in roster/education/sample.
Dropping loudly beats guessing quietly.

** cluster_features — MODE, not first (ties → NA)
=COVERN= is household-level (1,807 rows); =cluster_features= is declared at
=(t, v)=, so it must be reduced to one row per segment.  The declared reducer is
the *majority (mode)*.  With =v = (ED, SN)= the segment is 100% homogeneous in
=Rural= (0 of 168 ambiguous); =Region= is ambiguous in only 3 of 168 segments
(35 households), and in each the split is a lone stray household against the rest
(1-vs-10, 1-vs-11, 1-vs-11, all within one stratum and one sector) — a coding
error, not a real split.  =first()= would have let row order decide.  A genuine
tie has no majority and is set to =NA=, never guessed.

** sample — weight is a LOOKUP, not a spine
=WEIGHT.dta= is the 616-ED *national sampling frame*.  The framework merges
sub-dfs with =how='outer'=, so the 488 frame EDs that were never surveyed used to
arrive as phantom rows with =i = NaN= (1807 + 488 = 2295 rows).  The merge is now
keyed on a raw-ED helper column and the hook drops every i-less row.

EDs *408* and *482* (23 households) have no row in =WEIGHT.dta= at all; their
=weight= stays =NaN= — loudly missing, never imputed.

The weight is keyed on ED *in the source*: =WEIGHTID.dta= assigns one weight per
ED and 0 of its 126 EDs vary across their segments.  So looking the weight up on
ED while identifying the *cluster* as =(ED, SN)= is faithful to the survey —
joining =WEIGHT.dta= on ED reproduces =WEIGHTID='s household weights 1795/1795
with 100% value agreement.

* Traps

- *=HHCHAR.newid= is corrupt.*  Do NOT key =housing= on it: 319 duplicate values,
  and it disagrees with its own =(ed_dvsn, ed_smpl, smpl_hh)= triple in 240 of
  1,819 rows.  Where the two disagree the *triple* is the trustworthy one — it
  matches a real =COVERN= household in 235 of those 240 rows, versus 201 for
  =newid= (1,765 vs 1,731 overall).  (=COVERN.NEWID=, a different column in a
  different file, IS clean and unique, and =_/assets.py= does use it.)
- *=assets= sums.*  =_/assets.py= collapses per-acquisition detail rows with
  =groupby(['t','i','j']).sum()=.  That is only safe because =i= now names ONE
  real household; under the old =(ED, HH)= id the same sum silently pooled the
  durables of *two different households*, inventing holdings nobody reported.
  "We sum, so nothing is lost" is not by itself a safety argument.
- =EDUCN.dta= has 7,691 rows but =HS= (highest school) is NaN for 3,058 of them;
  those rows carry no education value and do not survive into
  =individual_education= (hence 4,633, not 7,691).  This is unrelated to #323.

* Known data issues

** shocks — not available (2026-06-06)
Guyana 1992 (early-design GLSS) has no shocks-and-coping roster. A scan of
every 1992 data file found no shock-type × impact × coping module. The
triage's candidate `DRBLS.dta` is the **durable-goods inventory** (item/year-
acquired/value triplets), already covered by `assets` — not shocks. The
closest signals (HEALTHN illness; HHCHAR other-income recall) are not shocks.
`shocks` is therefore not implementable. Tracked in GitHub issue #352.
