#+TITLE: Guyana — country notes & known data issues

* Household identity: the household is (ED, SN, HH)                    :GH503:

The Guyana 1992 household key is the **triple** =(ED, SN, HH)= — enumeration
district, sample number, household number.  =ED= alone, or =(ED, HH)=, is **not**
a household.

** The survey declares the key itself
=COVERN.dta= carries an explicit =NEWID= column, and

: NEWID == ED*100000 + SN*100 + HH

holds for **all 1807** cover-page rows.  (Check it in =int64=: the raw columns
are =int16= and the multiplication overflows.)  So the source itself says the
household is the triple.

| key            | distinct households in COVERN (1807 rows) |
|----------------+-------------------------------------------|
| =(ED, SN, HH)= |                                      1807 |
| =(ED, HH)=     |                                      1502 |

** What the old =(ED, HH)= key did (fixed 2026-07; GH #503)

Until this fix every Guyana table keyed on =i = [ED, HH]=.  That collapsed 1807
real households into 1502, and =_normalize_dataframe_index= silently resolved the
collision with =groupby().first()=.  The damage was **not** "missing rows" — it
was /wrong numbers that looked right/:

- **562** real households were merged into a shared row; **305** vanished from
  =sample()= entirely (1502 rows instead of 1807).
- **101 surviving households were chimeras** — they held members of two
  different real households — and **191 person-rows were grafted onto a
  household that was not theirs**.  Mean household size was inflated 4.38 →
  4.64.
- =assets= **summed two households' durables together**: 225 asset households
  were the arithmetic sum of two real households, yielding 220 =(i, j)= cells
  whose =Value= appears nowhere in the source.
- =interview_date= picked one of two households' dates arbitrarily for 305
  households.
- 888 person-rows were dropped from =household_roster=; 867 from
  =individual_education=.

Independent corroboration that the triple (not the pair) is the household:
=HHCHAR.hhsize= equals the =ROSTERN= member count for **98.1%** of households
keyed on =(ED, SN, HH)=, but only **81.1%** keyed on =(ED, HH)=.

Household ids are now =ED-SN-HH= strings (e.g. ='1-37-1'=, formerly ='1-1'=).
**Every Guyana number produced before this fix is suspect.**

Regression net: =tests/test_guyana_household_identity.py=.

* The sampling cluster is (ED, SN), not ED                             :GH503:

=ED= is not a cluster.  Across the 130 EDs, =RGN= varies within **22** of them,
=SECTOR= within **10**, =STNO= within **24**.  Since =Wave.cluster_features=
collapses to =(t, v)= with =.first()= ("invariant within a cluster by
construction of the LSMS-ISA sampling design"), =v: ED= handed **287 of 1807**
households a Region that is not their own and **143** the wrong Rural — which
then propagated into =m= / =market== aggregates.

Under =v = (ED, SN)= there are **168** clusters; =SECTOR= varies within **0** of
them and =RGN= within **3**.

** Residual: 13 households still get a wrong Region
=RGN= is inconsistent within 3 of the 168 =(ED, SN)= clusters, so the =.first()=
collapse still mis-assigns =Region= for **13** households (down from 287).  This
is a source inconsistency, not a key error; =cluster_features= is =(t, v)= by
canonical declaration, so fixing it properly means promoting Region to a
household-level column.  Not done.

** Weights stay keyed on ED
=WEIGHT.dta= is =(ED, WEIGHT)= with exactly one weight per ED, and joining it on
=ED= reproduces =WEIGHTID.dta='s authoritative per-household weight for
**1795/1795** rows.  So the =sample= weight sub-df keeps merging on a plain =ED=
helper column (dropped before the final index) even though =v= is now
=(ED, SN)=.  =WEIGHTID.dta= is /not/ a usable substitute: only 1742 of its 1795
triples are in COVERN, and it carries a duplicate triple.

2 of the 130 COVERN EDs (23 households) are absent from =WEIGHT.dta= and have a
NaN weight.  Pre-existing.

* =newid= is CORRUPT in the second-questionnaire files (HHCHAR, DRBLS) :GH503:

**Do not key =HHCHAR= or =DRBLS= on =newid=.**  Unlike =COVERN.NEWID=, the
=newid= column in the second-questionnaire family is malformed:

- Both files hold only **1616 distinct =newid= across ~1818 rows**.
- **240 of 1819** =HHCHAR= rows violate the =ED*100000 + SN*100 + HH= identity
  that =COVERN= satisfies exactly (the =HH= tail is mangled — e.g. ED 170 /
  SN 87 has =smpl_hh= 1,3,5,7,9,11 but =newid= tails 0,4,4,8,8,12).
- **317 =DRBLS= rows carry an ambiguous (duplicated) =newid=.**

Consequently the old =assets.py= join (=DRBLS.newid ↔ COVERN.NEWID=) recovered
only **1529 distinct households from 1730 rows** — it merged ~200 households
into others /even after/ the triple fix would have been applied to it.

** How =assets= recovers DRBLS's household: the HHCHAR positional crosswalk
=DRBLS= has no =ED=/=SN=/=HH= columns — only the corrupt =newid= and =id_nmbr=
(which is not a household key at all: 160 distinct values across 1818 rows).

=HHCHAR= carries **both** the corrupt =newid= /and/ the clean
=(ed_dvsn, ed_smpl, smpl_hh)= triple (== COVERN's =(ED, SN, HH)=).  After
dropping HHCHAR's single duplicate-triple record, HHCHAR is **row-for-row
parallel to DRBLS**: =newid= matches 1818/1818 /and/ =id_nmbr= matches 1818/1818.
So DRBLS row /k/ is HHCHAR row /k/'s household.

The crosswalk is validated where it can be: on the 1415 DRBLS rows whose =newid=
is /unambiguous/, it agrees with the old newid join for **1415/1415 (100%)**.  It
resolves the 317 rows the newid join cannot, and yields 1818 rows → **1818
distinct households** (1:1, as a wide one-row-per-household table must be).

=_/assets.py= **asserts** the row parity.  If a re-extract of either file breaks
it, the build raises rather than silently misattributing durables.

** No cross-household summation in =assets=
Because DRBLS is wide and each row is exactly one household, =(t, i, j)= is
unique by construction.  The old script collapsed duplicate =(t, i, j)= by
SUMMING, justifying it as "several per-acquisition detail rows for the same
(household, item)" — but that premise was itself an artifact of the conflation:
those "detail rows" were /different households/ colliding on =ED-HH=.  The only
legitimate within-household sum is blocks =31/31a/31b=, which share the item
label "OTHER AUDIO-VISUAL"; =assets.py= now raises if any /other/ item produces a
duplicate =(t, i, j)=.

* Known data issues

** housing — one irreducible key collision (1 of 1818 households)
=HHCHAR= contains two records that both claim ED 123 / SN 722 / HH 6.  They are
genuinely /different/ households (different sex of head, different income — 6847
vs 20785 — different walls and room count), and their =newid= is identical too,
so the collision cannot be resolved from the source.
=_normalize_dataframe_index= collapses them with =.first()= and emits the GH#323
=RuntimeWarning=.

**That warning is left in place on purpose.**  It is now /true and useful/ — one
genuine source defect — instead of drowning among the 311 spurious duplicates the
old key produced.  Silencing it would destroy the only detector we have for this
class of bug.

=housing= also carries 54 households whose triple is not in =COVERN= (out of
sample); they get a NaN =v=.

** shocks — not available (2026-06-06)
Guyana 1992 (early-design GLSS) has no shocks-and-coping roster. A scan of
every 1992 data file found no shock-type × impact × coping module. The
triage's candidate `DRBLS.dta` is the **durable-goods inventory** (item/year-
acquired/value triplets), already covered by `assets` — not shocks. The
closest signals (HEALTHN illness; HHCHAR other-income recall) are not shocks.
`shocks` is therefore not implementable. Tracked in GitHub issue #352.
