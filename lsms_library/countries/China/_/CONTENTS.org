#+title: Contents

* Overview

China Living Standards Survey (CLSS) conducted in Hebei and Liaoning
Provinces in July 1995 and July 1997.  One wave: 1995-97.

Source: https://doi.org/10.48529/ymjj-w136

* Sampling Design

** Not a probability sample

The BID states: "The CLSS sample is not a rigorous random sample
drawn from a well-defined population."

County and village selection was purposive (based on 1930s Japanese
survey sites), not random.  Only within-village household selection
used systematic random sampling from a household list.

Because the sample is non-probabilistic, *no sampling weights exist*
in the data files.  The =sample= table provides village-level PSU
identity (=v=) for clustering but weight/strata/Rural columns are
absent.

** PSU identification

Villages are the PSUs.  The household ID (=hid=) encodes geography:
- Digit 1: county (1--6)
- Digit 2: town within county (always 0)
- Digit 3: village within county (1--5)
- Digits 4--5: household within village

=v= is derived as =hid // 100= (3-digit village code, 30 villages).
Counties 1--3 map to Province 7 (Hebei); counties 4--6 to Province 8
(Liaoning).

** Structure

- 2 provinces, 6 counties, 31 villages (one county had an extra)
- ~880 farm households (787 in final data)
- 50 HH in each "main village" (1930s survey site), 20 HH in others
- Intended sample size: 780 (130 per county)
