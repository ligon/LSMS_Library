#+TITLE: Liberia — country notes & known data issues

* Survey identity
Liberia 2018-19 is the **National Household Forest Survey (NHFS) 2018-2019**
(LISGIS), a forest-livelihoods instrument — not a standard LSMS/HIES dwelling
survey. Confirmed from the WB DDI (catalog 3787) and the household
questionnaire (`liberia_hh_forestry_qx_final.pdf`).

* Known data issues

** cluster identity: use =ea_unique=, NEVER =ea_code= (2026-07-12, GH #323)
=sect1_public.dta= carries two EA-ish columns, and the obvious-looking one is
wrong:
- =ea_code= — an EA *serial number*, unique only within (county, district,
  clan). Just **32** distinct values, and it is a **dirty mixed-type column**
  (both ='132'= and =12.0= appear). It is merely the trailing field of
  =ea_unique=.
- =ea_unique= — the real EA key: **250** values, 10–13 households each, a string
  prefix of =hhid= for all 2,986 households. Region / county / district / clan /
  locality are each constant within it (zero violations).

=v= was originally wired to =ea_code=, so 250 real enumeration areas were merged
into 32 buckets. =ea_code= 12 alone held 621 households from 54 real EAs spanning
**all 14 counties**, and the =.first()= collapse in =Wave.cluster_features()=
stamped every one of them =county='bong'=. Silently WRONG, not merely missing.

Both =cluster_features.idxvars.v= and =sample.myvars.v= must stay on =ea_unique=,
**in lockstep** — =_join_v_from_sample()= pushes =sample='s =v= into every
household-level Liberia table, so changing one alone desynchronises the join and
yields all-NaN cluster attributes. Because =ea_unique= is float64 and =format_id=
is *not* auto-applied to myvars, =sample= needs the =v()= function in
=2018-19/_/mapping.py=; without it the two sides differ ='302003132.0'= vs
='302003132'= and match nothing.

Do **not** rebuild a composite key from (county, district, clan, ea_code): the
mixed dtypes split 9 real EAs in two (258 groups for 250 EAs). Use =ea_unique=.

The household→cluster reduction (2,986 rows → 250) is legitimate but is enforced
explicitly by the guarded =cluster_features= hook in =2018-19/_/mapping.py=, which
*asserts* attribute-constancy and raises. Note the =aggregation:= key in
=data_scheme.yml= is currently **inert** (nothing reads it), so it could not be
used for this.

** housing — not available (2026-06-06)
The NHFS questionnaire's 21-section table of contents has **no dwelling/housing
module**. The two plausibly-named sections are not dwelling characteristics:
- §18 "Household Access" = travel times to facilities (not physical attributes);
- §19 "Forests and Construction" = whether *forest/wild products* were used for
  construction (not roof/wall/floor materials).
A full-text scan of the questionnaire and a data-level sweep of all 27 section
files returned **zero** roof/wall/floor/toilet/water-source/electricity/rooms/
dwelling-tenure variables. `housing` is therefore not implementable for Liberia.
Tracked in GitHub issue #350.

(Note: the NHFS *does* carry §17 Shocks and §10 Land Parcels, so `shocks` and
`plot_features` remain implementable for Liberia — handled in their own batches.)
