#+title: Contents

* Overview

Niger has four waves in the LSMS Library: two pre-EHCVM national
surveys (2011-12, 2014-15) and two EHCVM waves (2018-19, 2021-22).

* Sampling Design

EHCVM convention (2018-19 and 2021-22): =grappe= is the cluster
identifier (=v=); each grappe is visited in exactly one =vague=, so
household identifier is =(grappe, menage)= without a vague level.
See CLAUDE.md "EHCVM countries" for the cross-country pattern.

Pre-EHCVM waves (2011-12, 2014-15) follow their own conventions;
see the wave-level =data_info.yml= files for index definitions.

* Household Presence / MonthsSpent

The EHCVM questionnaire (used in the 2018-19 and 2021-22 waves) does
NOT ask "How many months did [NAME] live here in the past 12 months?"
(the continuous MonthsSpent/MonthsAway variable available in Uganda,
Ethiopia, Tanzania, Malawi, and many other LSMS countries).  Instead,
EHCVM uses two binary eligibility filters:

- =s01q12= :: "Has [NAME] lived continuously in this household for at
  least 6 months?"
- =s01q13= :: "Does [NAME] intend to stay at least 6 months?"

These determine membership eligibility but do not give a continuous
duration measure.  Downstream analysts who need a presence filter for
=household_characteristics= should use these binary variables directly
rather than expecting a =MonthsSpent= column in =household_roster()=.

The pre-EHCVM waves DO have a continuous months-of-presence variable:

- 2011-12 :: =ms01q19= in the household roster file records the
  number of months the individual spent in the household over the
  past 12 months.  See the wave-level =data_info.yml= for details.
- 2014-15 :: =MS01Q19= in the household roster file records the
  number of months the individual spent in the household over the
  past 12 months.  See the wave-level =data_info.yml= for details.
